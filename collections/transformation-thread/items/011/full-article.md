# (Part 2 of 3) The New Divide in A.I.: The Cost of "Good Enough"


Yesterday I argued that the real advantage in A.I. is shifting from speed to judgment. That shift creates a new challenge.

>Most organizations won't struggle because they lack A.I.
>They'll struggle because they won't know when—or where—to trust it.

## The Emerging Bottleneck
Imagine five employees asking five different A.I. systems the same question. They receive five different answers. Which one becomes company policy? Who is responsible if the answer is wrong? These aren't technology questions. They're governance questions.

>## The Hidden Cost of "Good Enough"
>Many organizations are quietly accumulating a new kind of technical debt. Not software debt. Decision debt.
>- Every undocumented prompt...
>- Every unverified response...
>- Every assumption accepted without review...
...creates another decision someone will eventually have to revisit.
>
>The productivity gains are real. But so is the long-term cost when nobody can explain why an important decision was made. Eventually, organizations won't be judged by how much A.I. they use. They'll be judged by how confidently they can explain the decisions A.I. helped produce. On Monday, I'll look at why leadership—not technology—will become the defining competitive advantage in the A.I. era.
