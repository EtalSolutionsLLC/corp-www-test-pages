Automation can create speed. It can also create distance between a decision and the person responsible for it.

That is why accountability should come before scale.

Before automating a workflow, decide who owns the result. Define what happens when the normal path fails. Make exceptions visible. Preserve a review path for decisions that carry meaningful consequences. Record enough context that someone can understand why the system acted the way it did.

These are not reasons to avoid automation. They are the conditions that make automation durable.

The safest transformation programs do not ask only how much work can be automated. They ask where automation improves the work, where human judgment still matters, and how the organization will know when the system needs attention.
