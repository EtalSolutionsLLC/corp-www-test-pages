Transformation is supposed to make organizations more capable. It should not quietly discard the people who know how the work actually gets done.

Experience can become invisible in modern hiring and workforce systems. Automated screening may favor familiar titles, recent credentials, or a narrow version of career progression. Long experience can look less tidy on a form even when it is exactly what a difficult environment needs.

The same problem appears inside organizations. A modernization effort may focus on platforms and processes while overlooking the people who understand the history behind the exceptions.

A better approach treats experience as an asset to be translated, not a barrier to be filtered out. Pair institutional knowledge with new tools. Capture the reasons behind recurring workarounds. Make room for people who have seen several cycles of change and know which promises usually arrive with hidden costs.

Transformation should create more ways for experience to matter.
