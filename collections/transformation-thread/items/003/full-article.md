Transformation plans often become too large before they become useful. The organization names every problem, every system, every integration, and every future possibility. The document gets longer. The first move gets harder to see.

A smaller plan can be more serious.

Start with one operational pressure that people can recognize. Identify the process, system, or decision where that pressure is most visible. Choose a first move that improves the situation without requiring the organization to solve everything at once.

The smallest useful transformation plan should answer four questions. What is getting in the way? Who feels the friction? What evidence would show improvement? What can be changed without closing off better options later?

That is enough to begin. A small plan is not an excuse for shallow thinking. It is a way to make the thinking actionable.
